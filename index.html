What is lfg?

LFG is a combined gaming center and coworking space. Why would we combine these two? Because so much of what they do overlap and become better when they are together. Because what each is truly doing is looking for a group and build a community.

Gaming?

The larger percentage of LFG is dedicated to gaming space. We are providing PC setups where people can come and rent time (cheap, seriously!) to play games or browse the internet. We are also providing XBone and PS4 setups, both individual pods and several "living room" setups that are designed for single screen multiplayer parties.

Along with full setups, players will be able to rent just space where they can bring their own gaming rigs or table space to play pen and paper or board games. Many board games and gaming books will be provided by we also welcome and encourage new games to be played.

One of the special features of the gaming setup is the creation of custom servers for games. From Minecraft to Unreal Tournament to Chat (mattermost), LFG will host local servers to keep local stats, create local worlds and to let people build their own local community.

Coworking?

Coworking in Pittsburgh has been booming but there are no good coworking locations in the South Hills. LFG is offering desk space for a daily or monthly rental. This includes all of the now standard coworking amenities: coffee pot and fridge, all hours access, conference rooms, printer, scanner, great internet access and a community of people who are friendly and willing to talk.

But we are offering much more than that as well. Because the gaming side is offering computer rental, the coworking side is offering the same. This allows LFG coworking customers to be able to come in and do work even if they don't have a laptop handy. Or, more importantly, if they don't have the proper productivity software available on their machine.

Our productivity suite will include Office, Photoshop, Visual Studio, Inkscape and free software that will help members make their ideas a reality. We are also offering server based tools, such as VMs for testing, local web hosting for development testing and other server tools as requested.

Why Both?

As you can tell from some of the special offerings in the coworking space, the gaming portion of LFG adds a lot of value to the coworking space. But it isn't a one way street. By having a proper coworking space, LFG will be able to host community events, such as hacker days, professional learning series and developer meetups. The people who come to these events are often the exact same people who will want to spent time in the gaming center.

Pittsburgh has a growing game development community with Schell Games and CMU's Entertainment Technology Center as the backbone, but also has a long history with games with companies like eGenesis. By providing a coworking space attached to a gaming center, game developers have a great place to build and iterate designs and get immediate feedback from a passionate community.

Finally, coworking is a steady kind of revenue stream, while a gaming center can go through swings of high volume days or months and then lower volume. Pittsburgh has multiple colleges with likely gaming customers leaving for the summer, only to come back in the fall and pick up where they left off. Having a steady line of business will help smooth out the income stream and allow LFG to stay open more easily and not die over the summer lull.

What is special about LFG?

The people working on it. Seriously.

John is one of the owners and by day does system engineering for a large company, building out servers, systems, websites and other bits and bobs. In the evening, he does many of the same things along with playing and designing games. All of this requires a lot of technology and that technology requires a lot of knowledge. Most places would have to pay for the knowledge that John will apply and that knowledge isn't cheap, which is why many coworking and gaming centers don't offer the special offerings that LFG offers. It just so happens that along with this knowledge, comes a love of games and an amazingly thorough knowledge of video games that comes from running VGONetwork, a gaming news/reviews site, for years.

Edd is one of the owners and by day carries a gun and wears a bulletproof vest. He also has rebuilt houses and will talk for hours about the benefits of PEX tubing or spend those some hours talking about games and their design. Given the chance, he will have his life sucked into different games such as FTL, Payday 2 or Speedrunners. Edd is always looking for new board and card games, such as Snake Oil or a new version of Robo Rally, and sharing these games is one of his greatest passions.

There are also a few other, minor, differentiators: location, services, art installations, gaming installations (driving cockpits, golf simulators), owners who know people that can check our grammar.
